STEP 1: Write spec.md
Read CLAUDE.md in this directory first. Then write a design spec (spec.md) that meets these standards:

The spec MUST start with a genuine DESIGN CONCEPT — not a layout description. Look at the completed sites in CLAUDE.md for the bar:
- "The Watchmaker's Bench" (M&S Watch Repair)
- "The House Call" (Vivace Piano Service)
- "1959" (Say Ray Auto — built around founding year)
- "The Repair Counter" (Souren — claim-ticket/workbench metaphor)

These aren't "blue background + nice fonts." They have a POINT OF VIEW.

For Daniel: he's a plumber AND handyman. That's rare and it's his differentiator. Most homeowners don't know whether a problem needs a plumber or a handyman — Daniel removes that friction. He's also Nextdoor-referred only, meaning every job has to earn the next one. The concept should emerge from these truths.

The spec must include:
- Thesis paragraph explaining the concept and WHY it fits Daniel
- Design tokens (colors, type, layout) with JUSTIFICATION for each choice — not just "looks nice"
- Content outline
- Photo direction (Unsplash keywords since no real photos exist)

Do NOT write generic SaaS-landing-page structure. This is for a solo tradesman — the site should feel human, not corporate.
